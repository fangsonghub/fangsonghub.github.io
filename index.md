I am an Associate Professor in the [Computer Science Department](https://www.pdx.edu/computer-science/) at [Portland State University](https://www.pdx.edu/). My research lies at the intersection of **cryptography**, **quantum computing**, and computational complexity, including provable security against quantum attacks, quantum algorithms, and quantum pseudorandomness. I am grateful to the [National Science Foundation](https://www.nsf.gov/){:target="_blank"} (including a CAREER Award) and the [Sony Group](https://www.sony.com/en/SonyInfo/research-award-program/){:target="_blank"} for supporting my work. [[CV]({{ site.url }}/files/docs/cv_fs.pdf){:target="_blank"}]

Before joining Portland State, I was a postdoctoral fellow at the [Institute for Quantum Computing](http://iqc.uwaterloo.ca) and the [Department of Combinatorics and Optimization](http://math.uwaterloo.ca/combinatorics-and-optimization/) at the [University of Waterloo](http://uwaterloo.ca) (2013–2016). I received my PhD in [Computer Science and Engineering](http://www.cse.psu.edu/) from [Penn State](http://www.psu.edu) in 2013, advised by [Sean Hallgren](http://www.cse.psu.edu/~hallgren), and my bachelor's degree from [USTC](http://en.ustc.edu.cn/) in 2008.
